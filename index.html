Performance Engineering

Reviews Module — Item Page (PDP)

The Reviews component was taking ~380ms to render, causing visible lag on one of Walmart's highest-traffic pages. Delivered seven targeted optimizations to the rendering pipeline.

  • ViewStub lazy inflation — eliminated ~220ms of view construction by deferring inflation until the component is about to appear
  • Dirty-check guards — prevented unnecessary re-binds when underlying data had not changed
  • View pooling & adapter caching — reduced allocation churn and repeated lookups during fast scroll
  • Deferred analytics — moved event dispatch off the main thread, post-layout
  • Layout hierarchy flattening — reduced measure/layout pass cost across sub-components
380ms → 96ms render (−74%) CPU −72% Dropped frames −73% UI jank eliminated

Architecture

IRO Investigation — 7 Solutions, 5 Teams

UGC 2.0 needs item metadata dynamically per review page but has no internal catalog store. A single review page can reference up to 10 variant item IDs — only determinable after reviews are retrieved.

OptionFindingDecision
IQS4–8 week onboarding; Imperium rules not applied❌ Ruled out
P13N CassandraStores ML signals, not item metadata❌ Ruled out
Preso / SearchNo item-ID retrieval; depends on IRO❌ Ruled out
Item Service OL 4007Extra hop; same IRO dependency❌ Ruled out
GraphQL FederationDoes not reduce IRO traffic❌ Ruled out
UGC-owned storageOperational overhead without sufficient benefit❌ Ruled out
IRO + LRU CacheMirrors CLS production pattern; CCM-configurable TTL✅ Recommended

Engaged IQS, P13N, Preso/Search, Item Service OL, and IRO teams. Communicated full tradeoff analysis to 8 senior stakeholders.

UGC MegaCache — Proposed Design

Platform-wide Redis cache (~550k items, ~550MB, 1-day TTL) with Kafka event-driven invalidation + TTL fallback.
